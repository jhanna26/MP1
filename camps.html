Children Camp Activities
  • Field Day, Kids of all ages love Field Day where they can try their hands at all sorts of outdoor challenges.
  • Cavin Carnibal, Give kids time in the morning to brainstorm a fun carnival game they can create with things they find in their cabins or outside in nature.
  • JunkYard War, Based on an old TV series from the early 2000s, give campers a task to build a machine using only the items you give them.
  • Myth Buster,Comparing fingerprints using an ink pad and paper.Getting campers to yawn to see if it’s really contagious.Setting a table with second hand plates to see if you can really pull a table close out without breaking anything.

Music Camp Activities
  • Children can learn note values, the musical alphabet or how to read music in a classroom, but music camp can enhance the experience of doing so. Review note values, such as quarter, half and whole notes and rests
  • Teach students about these instruments -- how they make sound, what family of instrument they fit into and how to play them.
  • Get kids active with music and movement activities. Younger children will love freeze dances or songs that instruct them to move a certain way, such as jumping, spinning or doing the twist.
  • Make a musical scavenger hunt, and the first team to find and correctly name all the items wins.
Cancer Camp Activities
  • Peer support program for parents and caregivers of children going through cancer treatment and long-term cancer care.
  • A weekend getaway for teens diagnosed with cancer to come together and share their unique experiences and talk about their emotional, physical and social adjustments they have undergone.
  • Week-long overnight summer camp for children afflicted with disabilities and/or medical conditions ages 6-18.
  • Spring and Fall weekend retreats for individuals 18 and older with cancer.
